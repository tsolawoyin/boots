Virus

  • A virus is extremely small(smaller than every other living organism) and can only be seen through an electron microscope
  • It does not have a cell strucure. A virus only has either RNA or DNA inside a protein coat.
  • A virus can be regarded as a living thing and a non-living thing
  • A virus is living if and only if it is inside a living organism e.g Pig
  • A virus is non-living if it is not inside a living organism e.g. table

Kingdom Monerans

  • The bacteria and the blue-green algae belong to this kingdom
  • They are very small organisms and are made up of single prokaryotic cells enclosed within rigid cell walls.
  • The cell structure is simple. They have no definite nucleus and the chromosome lie free in a particular part of the cell.
Bacteria (singular: bacterium)
  • Bacteria can be seen through the high power of a light microscope
  • They are found everywhere including inside your body and inside hot volcanoes
  • Some bacteria look like sphere and are called Cocci
  • Some bacteria look like rod and are called Bacillus
  • Some are coiled or look like snake and are called Spirochaetae
  • A few type make their food through a process called Chemosynthesis
  • Some are Saprophyte i.e they feed on dead organisms causing them to decay.
  • Some live and feed on living organisms, harming them. Such bacteria are called Parasites.
  • Bacteria reproduce only asexually by binary fission
Blue-green algae
  • Are also referred to as cyano bacteria
  • They are found mainly in freshwater, and in damp places on land.
  • They contain chlorophyll and so can make their own food through Photosynthesis
  • The chlorophyll is not located inside the chloroplast.
  • They exist as single cell and colonies of cells
  • Examples include: Nostoc, Oscillatoria and Anabaena

Kingdom Protista

  • Protists are unicellular organisms(i.e they are made of one cell)
  • They have an eukaryotic cell type
  • Their cells have a true nucleus and organelles.
  • The protists are much bigger than a bacteria
  • They live in water, which include rivers, seas, moist places and body fluids
  • Many of them move about with structures like flagella and cilia or by forming pseudopodium, also known as false feet

There are two major types of protists

  • Protophyta (Protists that have plant-like feature/characterisitcs)
  • Protozoa (Protists that have animal-like feature/characterisitcs)
Protophyta
  • They have cellulose cell walls
  • They possess chloroplasts which contains chlorophyll, hence they can photosynthesize or make their own food
  • Examples include Chlamydomonas, Chlorella, and Diatoms
Protozoa
  • They have no cellulose cell walls
  • They have no chlorophyll, hence cannot photosynthesize
  • Examples include Amoeba, Paramecium

Important Protists

About Euglena viridis
  • A special protist that have both plant-like and animal-like features.
  • It has chlorophyll which enables it to make its own food (plant-like). It is an autotroph
  • It lacks a cellulose cell wall (animal like)
  • It has a plasma membrane called Pellice (animal-like)
  • It has eyespot or stigma which is sensitive to light
  • It moves with the aid of flagellum
  • Possess contractile vacoule for osmoregulation
