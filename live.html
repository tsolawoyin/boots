About Boots

In reality, Boots wasn't a planned or designed web application. Boots is merely the outcome of random nature. Boots was created in response to our little study group's requirement for software to practice questions and other things. Yes, there are a ton of websites and apps available to assist us with practicing, but I regret to say that I disagree with the majority of them's underlying attitude. Yes, some provide a vast question bank, charts, graphs, and even competitions with cash awards. These programs are helpful, but they are not meant to assist in the development of completely functional brains. I think they are more focused on making money. I therefore set out to design BOOTS in order to close these gaps.

What makes Boots different

Alright. First and foremost, Boots is free and it will be free for a very long time. I decided to make Boots open source, GNU GPL-3.0 licence, with the hope that I will get other programmers and volunteers alike to collaborate and together we will make Boots better.

Secondly, Boots is like other practice software you are familiar with, with slight modifications.

  • Instant marking as opposed to marking when exam is finished. This I believe will force users to make corrections immediately.
  • User score is displayed right after answering each questions.
  • Score increases if user answer the current question correctly
  • Score reduces if user answers wrongly. Simply put there is negative marking.
  • Score is unaffected if user doesn't attempt the questions. That is, you won't be rewarded or penalized for questions you don't answer.
  • And the rest will be learned when you start using the software.

Thirdly, Boots is not a JAMB practice app. Boots is a multipurpose practice software. I developed the software in order to practice any form of test. MCQs, theory, German questions e.t.c. But for now, most of the questions in Boots are JAMB questions. Most especially in English and Biology. Chemistry is a mixture of JAMB questions and questions from textbooks. But this is just as it is right now. Boots is roughly a month old, as it grows, this will change.
