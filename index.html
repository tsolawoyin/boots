    Air

    Please focus on;

    1. the natural gaseous constituents - nitrogen, oxygen, water vapor, carbon(IV) oxide and the noble gases(argon, neon, helium, radon, xenon, and krypton)
    2. air as a mixture and some uses of noble gas

    Water

    Under this topic, you should focus your attention on;

    1. Composition by volume of air in water
    2. Water as a solvent, atmospheric gases dissolved in water and their biological significance.
    3. Water as a product of the combustion of hydrogen.
    4. Hard and soft water
    5. Temporary and permanent hardness and methods of softening hard water.
    6. Purification of town water supplies.
    7. Water of crystallization, efflorescence, deliquescence and hygroscopy.
    8. Examples of the substances exhibiting these properties and their uses

    Environmental pollution

    Focus on:

    1. Sources and effects of pollutants.
    2. Air pollution: Examples of air pollutants such H2S, CO, SO2, oxides of nitrogen, fluorocarbons and dust
    3. Water pollution: Sewage and oil pollution
    4. Soil pollution: Oil spillage, Biodegradable and non-biodegradable pollutants,

    Carbon

    Reading guide (pages included if you are using Understanding chemistry)

    • Allotropes of carbon: their properties and uses. (page 266 - 268. Stop at carbon black)
    • Carbon(IV) oxide:
      • laboratory prep, properties and uses. (pg 269 - 271)
      • action of heat on trioxocarbonate(IV) salts. (pg 275, property 3)
      • test for CO32- ions. (pg 275, property 5)
    • Carbon(II) oxide:
      • laboratory preparation, properties including its effect on blood. (pg 272 - 274. Stop at Effect on CO on the blood)
      • sources of carbon(II) oxide to include charcoal, fire and exhaust fumes.
    • Coal: different types, products obtained from destructive distillation of wood and coal. (pg 278 - 279. Stop at Destructive distillation of wood)
    • Coke: gasification and uses, manufacture of synthesis gas and uses. (pg 277)

    Sulfur

    Please focus on the following only. Don't stress yourself

    1. Uses and allotropes of sulfur. (preparation of allotropes are not expected/needed!)
    2. Preparation and uses of sulphur(IV) oxide
    3. The reaction of SO2 with alkalis.
    4. Trioxosulphate(IV) acid and its salts
    5. The effect of acids on salts of trioxosulphate(IV) acid
    6. Tetraoxosulphate(VI) acid
      • commercial preparation (contact process only)
      • properties as a dilute acid
      • an oxidizing and a dehydrating agent and uses. test for SO42-
    7. Hydrogen sulphide:
      • Praparation and properties as a weak acid
      • Reducing agent and precipitating align-center
